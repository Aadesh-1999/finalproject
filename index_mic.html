Speech to Sketch

Abstract

Language and images are closely intertwined: We think in pictures and we explain facts as spatial constellations. A picture is worth a thousand words, but sometimes thousand words are also not enough when the opposite person is unable to understand us properly. A lot of times only words and sentences are not enough to explain something to physically challenged or to small kids. At everyoneâ€™s childhood, grandparents or may be parents tells the stories to kids. After telling something most of time words are difficult to visualize for kids. Images can be better understood by human brain. What if the spoken word could be transformed into dynamic visual worlds in real time? Speech input, machine learning and recurrent neural networks for image generation allow to computer generate complex imaginary worlds that follow the narrator and thus create complex animations controlled by linguistic structures. Hence we have proposed and working on a system which will help mentally disable people or physically challenged people like deaf and also will enable kids to understand the context more easily by helping them to visualize the sentences in the form of animation of sketches.
Technical Keywords: Artificial Intelligence, Speech Recognition, Natural Language Processing, Deep Learning, Gesture, Image Processing, Machine Learning, Object Recognition, Neural Networks.
